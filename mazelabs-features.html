Privacy Framing

LLMs should not see raw sensitive infrastructure documents by default. MazeLabs should provide controlled, masked, compressed, evidence-based context instead of dumping full documents and logs into the model.

Compress noisy telemetry into evidence LLMs can actually use.

Raw logs are too noisy, repetitive, and expensive for LLMs to process directly. MazeLabs includes a Log Compressor that reduces high-volume telemetry into structured evidence, incident signals, anomalies, timelines, entities, symptoms, and candidate failure points.

This is a core differentiator: Generic LLMs struggle with massive logs. MazeLabs compresses operational telemetry first, then lets AI reason over clean evidence instead of raw noise.

  • Deduplicate repeated log lines
  • Extract error clusters
  • Identify time windows and spike patterns
  • Detect service, endpoint, host, region, database, queue, and dependency entities
  • Build incident timelines
  • Preserve source references
  • Summarize symptoms without losing critical signals
  • Convert raw logs into Evidence Packs
  • Reduce token usage and model confusion
  • Improve privacy by removing unnecessary raw context

Mask sensitive data before AI reasoning.

MazeLabs should include a privacy layer that detects and masks sensitive operational information before evidence is passed to model providers or simulation agents. The platform should prioritize customer control over what is exposed, retained, or used.

  • Mask secrets, tokens, keys, IPs, hostnames, emails, customer IDs, internal URLs, and sensitive identifiers
  • Keep raw source documents in the vault
  • Pass only bounded, masked, simulation-relevant evidence to AI components
  • Maintain source provenance without exposing unnecessary raw data
  • Support configurable masking policies
  • Reduce the blast radius of AI provider exposure

Measure operational capability, not course completion.

MazeLabs scores how teams investigate, decide, communicate, escalate, and recover. It is designed to measure operational readiness rather than passive learning.

Every simulation ends with a structured debrief. Teams can review what they saw, what they missed, what they did, what they should have done, and how their response compared to the expected evidence path.
